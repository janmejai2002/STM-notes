institutions. These institutions persisted to the present. Exploiting differences in

Rivalry among competitors' 
•Beyond intensity of competition, the grounds of competition is important
•Prompt: Where competition is happening for coke and pepsi ?

•Are two firms competing on same dimensions or different?

Resource Based View (RBV)
•‘Resources are key to superior performance’ ( Wernerfelt , 1984).
•Looking at firms in terms of their resources leads to different immediate insights than 
the traditional product perspective. In particular, diversified firms are seen in a new 
light. 
•One can identify types of resources which can lead to high profits. In analogy to entry 
barriers, these are associated with what can be referred as ‘resource position 
barriers’. 
•Strategy for a bigger firm involves striking a balance between the exploitation of 
existing resources and the development of new ones. 
•In analogy to the growth -share matrix, Wernerfelt  argues for a resource -product matrix. 
•An acquisition can be seen as a purchase of a bundle of resources in a highly 
imperfect market. By basing the purchase on a rare resource, one can ceteris paribus 
maximize this imperfection and one's chances of buying cheap and getting good 
returns

A Rare Resource
•A resource is rare if only few firms posses it.
•If resource is common then – perfect competition
•If resource is valuable but not rare – competitive parity
•For competitive advantage, the valuable resource has to be rare,
